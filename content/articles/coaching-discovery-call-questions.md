A discovery call is not an interview. It is not a pitch. It is a diagnosis. 

Imagine walking into a doctor’s office with a sharp pain in your stomach. If the doctor immediately says, *"I have this great $5,000 surgery package, want to buy it?"* you would run out the door. The doctor has to ask questions, probe the pain, understand the symptoms, and *then* prescribe the solution. 

As a coach, you must do exactly the same thing. The problem is that most coaches rely on surface-level **coaching discovery call questions** like *"What are your goals?"* or *"Tell me about your business."* These elicit surface-level, logical answers. 

People do not buy coaching based on logic. They buy based on emotion. They buy to escape pain or move toward a profound desire. To uncover those emotions, you need a psychological arsenal of **coaching qualifying questions**. 

In this guide, we will break down the framework for asking the right questions, give you 27 specific questions you can use today, and show you how to transition from asking questions to confidently stating your price. 

## Why Asking the Right Coaching Discovery Call Questions Matters

If you are selling a high-ticket program, your closing rate depends entirely on your ability to build a "Gap." The Gap is the distance between the prospect’s Current State (where they are struggling) and their Desired State (where they want to be). 

If the Gap is small, they won't pay $10,000 to fix it. If the Gap is massive, the price becomes irrelevant. Your **coaching sales questions** are the shovels you use to dig that Gap. 

For [executive coaching professionals](/coaches/executive-coaching), asking the right questions is also a matter of frame control. High-level executives are used to being in charge. If you don't control the flow of the conversation with authoritative questioning, they will hijack the call and start interviewing *you*. 

## The "Gap" Framework: 4 Categories of Coaching Qualifying Questions

Every question you ask should fall into one of four categories. You must move sequentially through these categories. Do not jump to "Commitment" questions before you have fully explored the "Current State."

### Category 1: Current State (The Pain)
You need to understand exactly what is broken right now. 
1. "What motivated you to book this call with me today specifically?"
2. "Walk me through what a typical day looks like for you right now."
3. "What is the biggest bottleneck in your life/business currently?"
4. "How long has this specific issue been going on?"
5. "What impact is this problem having on your personal life/mental health/family?"
6. "On a scale of 1-10, how frustrated are you with where things are at right now?"
7. "What have you already tried to do to fix this on your own?"

### Category 2: Future State (The Desire)
Once you understand the pain, you must help them visualize the outcome. 
8. "If we were having this conversation 12 months from now, what needs to have happened for you to feel like it was a wildly successful year?"
9. "Let’s wave a magic wand and fix [Pain Point]. What does your life look like the next morning?"
10. "Why is hitting that specific goal so important to you?" *(Keep asking "why" to dig deeper).*
11. "How would achieving that revenue/lifestyle goal change things for your family?"
12. "What is the very first thing you would do if you hit [Specific Goal]?"
13. "What does success actually mean to you at this stage of your life?"
14. "If you achieved this, how would it change how you view yourself?"

### Category 3: Roadblocks (The Resistance)
Now that the Gap is established, you must figure out why they haven't crossed it yet. This uncovers hidden objections.
15. "You are clearly smart and driven. Why do you think you haven't been able to achieve this on your own yet?"
16. "What is the biggest fear you have about trying to solve this problem?"
17. "If nothing changes, and you are in the exact same spot 5 years from now, what does that cost you?"
18. "Have you ever worked with a coach or consultant before? What was that experience like?"
19. "Do you feel like you have a strategy problem, an execution problem, or a mindset problem right now?"
20. "What do you think is the biggest missing piece of the puzzle?"

### Category 4: Commitment (The Close)
Finally, use **coaching qualifying questions** to gauge if they are actually ready to invest money and time. 
21. "Is solving this problem a 'nice-to-have' right now, or an absolute 'must-have'?"
22. "Are you willing to be uncomfortable and do things differently than you've done them in the past?"
23. "If I told you I have a framework that bridges the gap between where you are and where you want to be, are you in a position to invest in your growth today?"
24. "Other than yourself, is there anyone else who needs to be involved in a financial decision of this magnitude?" *(Crucial B2B qualifying question).*
25. "On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you to fixing this right now?"
26. "If the money wasn't an issue, is this something you are 100% ready to tackle?"
27. "Based on everything we've discussed, would you like me to walk you through exactly how I can help?"

## How to Ask Coaching Qualifying Questions Without Sounding Like an Interrogation

Reading a list of 27 **coaching discovery call questions** out loud will make you sound like a robot. You must use active listening. 

When a prospect answers a question, do not immediately read the next question on your list. Use a technique called "mirroring." Repeat the last three words of their sentence back to them with an upward inflection. 

*Prospect:* "I just feel like I'm constantly putting out fires and I have no time."
*You:* "No time?"
*Prospect:* "Yeah, because my team keeps making the same mistakes, so I have to fix them."

You just uncovered a deeper pain point without asking a formal question.
