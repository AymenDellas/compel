## The Psychology of the Tripwire Strategy for Coaches

In the digital marketing world, acquiring a new customer is infinitely more difficult—and expensive—than selling to an existing one. The hardest barrier to break in any coaching business is getting a prospect to pull out their credit card for the very first time. 

If you are a high-ticket coach offering a $5,000 or $10,000 package, expecting cold traffic to immediately book a call and wire you thousands of dollars is a massive leap of faith. This is where the **tripwire funnel coaching** framework becomes the ultimate secret weapon for scaling.

A tripwire is an irresistible, incredibly low-priced offer (typically between $7 and $47). Its purpose is not to make you rich. Its purpose is to change the relationship. When a prospect transitions from a "free subscriber" to a "paying customer"—even if it's only for $27—a psychological shift occurs. They have voted with their wallet. They have demonstrated intent. They are now mathematically proven to be significantly more likely to purchase your high-ticket services.

If you have ever wondered [why your funnel is leaking](/learn/why-funnel-leaking) and why your lead magnets aren't resulting in sales calls, implementing a low ticket coaching offer might be the exact mechanism you need to bridge the gap.

## How a Tripwire Liquidates Your Ad Spend

Before diving into the architecture of the funnel, you must understand the economics. 

Let's say you are running Facebook ads directly to a free lead magnet. It costs you $10 to acquire a lead. You spend $1,000, you get 100 leads. You then have to rely entirely on your backend [email sequence](/learn/coaching-email-nurture-sequence) to book calls and eventually make a high-ticket sale to recoup that $1,000. This requires cash flow and patience.

Now, imagine you add a $27 tripwire immediately after the free lead magnet opt-in. 
- You spend $1,000 on ads and get 100 leads.
- 15% of those leads purchase the $27 tripwire on the next page.
- 15 buyers x $27 = $405 in immediate revenue.
- Now, your net ad spend is only $595 instead of $1,000. 

If you add an Order Bump (a $37 add-on at checkout) and a One-Time Offer (a $97 upsell on the thank you page), you can often make back the entire $1,000 immediately. This is called a "Self-Liquidating Offer." 

When your ad spend is liquidated instantly, your customer acquisition cost drops to zero. You can afford to outspend your competition indefinitely.

## The Architecture of a High-Converting Coaching Tripwire Funnel

The structure of a tripwire funnel requires precise execution. It relies on capitalizing on momentum and high intent.

### Step 1: The Two-Step Opt-In
The funnel usually begins with a standard, highly desirable free lead magnet (as discussed in our guide on [coaching lead magnet funnels](/learn/coaching-lead-magnet-funnel)). 

They land on your squeeze page and enter their email address to claim the free resource.

### Step 2: The Tripwire Sales Page (The Pivot)
Instead of directing them to a standard "Thank You" page, you immediately redirect them to the Tripwire Sales Page.

The copy on this page must acknowledge the free gift while instantly pivoting to the paid offer.
*Example Headline:* "Wait! Your free Executive Resume Template is on its way to your inbox... But before you go, read this page to discover how you can get my full Interview Mastery Toolkit for 80% off."

The tripwire offer must be a "splinter" of your core offering. If you provide [executive coaching](/coaches/executive-coaching) focused on leadership, your tripwire could be a comprehensive vault of difficult conversation scripts or a leadership audit framework. It must be highly actionable, incredibly valuable, and priced so low it feels like a steal. 

### Step 3: The Order Form and Order Bump
When they click to buy the $27 tripwire, they land on the checkout page. Here, you must include an **Order Bump**.

An order bump is a small checkbox on the checkout form offering a complementary product for a slightly higher price (e.g., $37 or $47). 
*Example:* "Check this box to add the 'Negotiation Playbook Audio Course' to your order for just $37! This normally sells for $197."

Because their credit card is already out and they are in buying mode, 20-40% of buyers will check this box, drastically increasing your Average Order Value (AOV).

### Step 4: The One-Time Offer (OTO) / Upsell
After they complete the purchase, do not send them to the receipt page yet. Direct them to a One-Time Offer (OTO) page.

This is an offer for a mid-ticket product, usually priced between $97 and $297. It must be a logical next step. If they bought the templates (the tripwire), the OTO might be a 90-minute masterclass on exactly how to deploy those templates. 

**Critical Feature:** Use "One-Click Upsell" technology. Because you already securely captured their credit card on the previous step, they only have to click a single button to add the $197 item to their order. No need to re-enter details. This dramatically increases conversion rates.

### Step 5: The Ascension to High-Ticket Coaching
Once they complete the buying sequence, they land on the final Thank You/Receipt page. 

This is where you bridge the gap to your high-ticket offer. Include a video welcoming them to your ecosystem, explaining how to access their purchases, and planting the seed for your premium coaching.

"Now that you have the tools, you have two choices. You can implement this yourself, or you can get my personal guidance to guarantee your results. Click below to book a free strategy session."

## Crafting the Perfect Tripwire Offer

The success of a tripwire strategy for coaches relies entirely on the quality of the product. 

**It must be deeply discounted.** If it is a $27 product, it needs to look, feel, and function like a $200 product. You are over-delivering intentionally to build massive trust. If they are blown away by your $27 offer, they will naturally conclude, "If her cheap stuff is this good, her $5,000 [career coaching](/coaches/career-coaching) program must be incredible."

**It must solve an immediate problem.** Do not sell a theoretical philosophy. Sell templates, swipe files, calculators, exact scripts, or standard operating procedures (SOPs). They should be able to consume it and use it within an hour.

## The Backend Email Nurture

Not everyone who buys the tripwire will immediately book a high-ticket discovery call. That is where your automated email sequences take over. 

You must separate your list into two segments: non-buyers and buyers.

**For Non-Buyers (Free Lead Magnet Only):**
Send a sequence attempting to sell them the tripwire they missed, or provide more value to build trust before asking them to book a call. 

**For Buyers (The Golden List):**
These are your hottest leads. Treat them like VIPs. Send an onboarding sequence checking in on their progress with the tripwire product. Once they have experienced the value, transition into a pitch for a discovery call. Share high-level case studies showing what happens when clients move from the basic tools (the tripwire) to full implementation (your coaching).
