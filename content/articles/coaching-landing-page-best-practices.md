## Key Takeaways

*   **Focus on the prospect, not yourself:** Your page must speak to the client's pain points and desired outcomes, not just your coaching philosophy.
*   **Remove all exit routes:** The best coaching pages have zero navigation menus and only one Call to Action (CTA).
*   **Optimize for mobile first:** Over 70% of traffic, especially from social platforms, will view your page on a mobile device.
*   **Speed is critical:** A page that takes longer than 3 seconds to load will lose up to 40% of its visitors before they even read the headline.
*   **Align your messaging:** If you are [scaling on LinkedIn](/learn/scaling-linkedin), your landing page must perfectly match the promise made in your LinkedIn post.

## The 9-Point Coaching Landing Page Checklist

If you are a [career coach](/coaches/career-coaching) trying to help executives transition, or a [life coach](/coaches/life-coaching) helping individuals find purpose, the psychological triggers required to make someone book a call remain identical. Follow this 9-point checklist to dramatically improve your coaching conversion rates.

### 1. The "Rule of One" (One Audience, One Offer, One CTA)
The most common mistake coaches make is trying to be everything to everyone. Your landing page must adhere strictly to the "Rule of One." 
*   **One Audience:** Speak directly to a specific avatar. Don't say "I help people." Say "I help VP-level executives."
*   **One Offer:** You are not selling your course, your book, and your coaching program on the same page. You are offering one thing: a [discovery call](/learn/coaching-discovery-call-script).
*   **One CTA:** Remove the header navigation, the footer links, and the social media icons. Every button on the page must say the exact same thing (e.g., "Book Your Strategy Session"). If you give a visitor five options, they will choose none of them.

### 2. The High-Impact "Above the Fold" Section
"Above the fold" refers to what a visitor sees before they have to scroll down. You have approximately three seconds to convince them to stay. This section must include:
*   **A Benefit-Driven Headline:** Your headline should not be "Welcome to John Doe Coaching." It should be "Scale Your Agency to $50k/Month Without Working Weekends."
*   **A Supporting Sub-headline:** Expand on the headline, explaining *how* you deliver the result.
*   **A High-Contrast CTA Button:** The button to book a call should be the brightest, most obvious element on the screen.
*   **Immediate Proof:** Include a small banner of logos (e.g., "Featured in," or logos of companies your clients work for) immediately below the button.

### 3. Hyper-Specific Objection Handling
Your prospects are reading your page with extreme skepticism. They have likely been burned by other coaches before. High-converting coaching landing pages systematically dismantle objections through the copy. 
Identify the top three reasons someone would *not* book a call with you, and address them directly. 
*   *Objection:* "I don't have time." -> *Copy Fix:* "Our framework requires only 2 hours of implementation per week."
*   *Objection:* "Will this work for my specific industry?" -> *Copy Fix:* "Designed exclusively for B2B SaaS founders."
Do not hide from objections. Bring them to the surface and destroy them.

### 4. The Value Proposition of the Call Itself
Most coaches treat the discovery call as a sales pitch, and prospects know this. No one wants to book a 45-minute sales pitch. 
You must sell the *value of the call*, not just the coaching program. Frame the call as a valuable, standalone diagnostic session. 
Instead of "Book a Free Consultation," use phrasing like:
*   "Claim Your Free Revenue Audit"
*   "Book Your Leadership Strategy Mapping Session"
*   "Get Your Customized 90-Day Action Plan"
Make it clear that they will walk away with massive value, even if they never pay you a dime.

### 5. Multi-Layered Social Proof
Testimonials are good, but multi-layered social proof is better. A single quote block with no photo is no longer sufficient. You need to stack proof elements:
*   **Video Testimonials:** Unpolished, authentic videos of clients talking about their results are the gold standard of social proof.
*   **Data-Driven Case Studies:** Don't just say "John felt better." Say "John increased his pipeline by 43% in 60 days."
*   **Screenshot Proof:** Real screenshots of Stripe dashboards, client emails, or Slack messages (with permission) provide undeniable, visceral proof that your system works.

### 6. Relentless Mobile Optimization
Look at your analytics. It is highly likely that the vast majority of your traffic is viewing your landing page on a phone. Coaching page optimization means designing for mobile *first*.
*   **Button Placement:** Is your CTA button easily clickable with a thumb?
*   **Text Size:** Is the text legible without zooming in? Break up long paragraphs into single sentences. 
*   **Media Loading:** Ensure images and videos are compressed so the page loads instantly on a 4G connection. 
If your page is clunky on mobile, you are losing more than half of your potential pipeline.

### 7. The Frictionless Booking Flow
The harder it is to book a call, the fewer calls you will book. Your landing page must seamlessly integrate with your calendar software (Calendly, Acuity, etc.).
*   **Direct Embeds:** Whenever possible, embed the calendar directly on the page rather than linking out to a third-party site. This removes a crucial step and keeps the user immersed in your ecosystem.
*   **Pre-Qualifying Questions:** It is a best practice to ask 3-5 questions before the booking is confirmed to ensure the prospect is qualified. However, keep these questions easy to answer. If you ask for their life story in a text box, they will abandon the form.

### 8. Message Match and Contextual Continuity
If a user clicks an ad or a post on LinkedIn that says, "How to fix your public speaking anxiety," and they land on a page that says, "Comprehensive Executive Coaching for Leaders," they will immediately bounce. This is called a failure of message match.
The headline on your landing page must perfectly mirror the copy of the link they just clicked. If you run multiple campaigns with different angles, you need multiple, dedicated landing pages. Contextual continuity ensures the prospect feels they are in the exact right place.

### 9. The "Cost of Inaction" (COI) Section
People are motivated by the desire to gain, but they are *hyper-motivated* by the fear of loss. Near the bottom of your landing page, you must articulate the Cost of Inaction. 
What happens if they *don't* book this call? What will their life or business look like in 12 months if they continue on their current trajectory?
Paint a vivid, painful picture of the status quo. Show them that doing nothing is actually the most expensive and dangerous choice they can make.

## Implementation: Fixing the Leaks

Knowing these coaching landing page best practices is useless without implementation. To begin, do a brutally honest audit of your current page. 
Ask a friend (or better yet, a former client) to look at your page for 5 seconds and then close their eyes. Ask them: "What exactly do I do, who do I do it for, and what do I want you to click?" If they cannot answer flawlessly, your page is leaking revenue.
