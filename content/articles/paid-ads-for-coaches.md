## Key Takeaways

*   **Paid Ads Amplify, They Don't Fix:** If your offer doesn't convert organically, paid ads will only help you lose money faster.
*   **Intent vs. Interruption:** Google Ads target high intent (people searching for a coach), while Facebook/LinkedIn target interruption (putting an offer in front of passive scrollers). Both require vastly different strategies.
*   **The Funnel is More Important Than the Ad:** A great ad sending traffic to a bad landing page will fail. Your backend funnel must be optimized.
*   **Beware of the Retainer:** When hiring an agency, understand the risks of the [retainer vs performance](/learn/retainer-vs-performance) models.

## The Prerequisite for Coaching Ads

Before you spend a single dollar on Mark Zuckerberg’s platform, you must have two things locked in:

1.  **A Validated High-Ticket Offer:** If you are selling a $97 course, paid ads will rarely be profitable due to the rising costs of customer acquisition. You need a high-ticket offer (typically $2,000+) so that you have the margin to acquire a customer profitably.
2.  **A Proven Conversion Mechanism:** You must know that if you get 10 qualified people on a discovery call, you can close at least 2 or 3 of them. If your sales skills are lacking, ads will not save you.

## Facebook and Instagram Ads: The Volume Play

When people think of paid ads for coaches, they immediately think of Facebook and Instagram. Despite the iOS 14 privacy updates, Meta's algorithm remains arguably the most powerful machine learning tool for finding buyers.

### Best For:
*   Life Coaches
*   Fitness/Health Coaches
*   Mindset Coaches
*   [Business Coaching](/coaches/business-coaching) (specifically targeting small business owners/solopreneurs)

### The Strategy:
Because Facebook is an interruption platform, you cannot simply run an ad that says "Book a $5,000 Coaching Package." People are on Facebook to see photos of their friends, not to buy high-ticket services.

You must use a multi-step funnel:
1.  **The Ad:** A highly engaging video or long-form copy post that calls out a specific pain point.
2.  **The Lead Magnet:** The ad drives traffic to a free, highly valuable resource (a checklist, a short training video, or a quiz).
3.  **The Upsell/VSL:** Immediately after they opt-in, they are presented with a Video Sales Letter (VSL) that bridges the gap between the freebie and your paid coaching, pushing them to book a call.
4.  **The Nurture:** A 7-day automated [email sequence](/learn/coaching-email-nurture-sequence) follows up with case studies and urgency to catch those who didn't book immediately.

## Google Ads: The Intent Engine

Google Ads (Search campaigns) operate on an entirely different philosophy. You aren't interrupting anyone; you are answering a direct request. When a CEO types "executive leadership coach NYC," they have incredibly high intent. They know what their problem is, and they know the solution they want.

### Best For:
*   Executive Coaches
*   Career Coaches
*   Specialized Business Coaches (e.g., "Agency Scaling Coach")

### The Strategy:
Clicks on Google Ads are expensive. A click for a highly competitive keyword might cost $10 to $20. Therefore, you cannot send this traffic to a cheap lead magnet.

1.  **High-Intent Keywords:** Target "bottom of funnel" keywords. Avoid broad terms like "leadership." Target specific phrases like "executive coaching for tech founders."
2.  **Direct to Call Funnels:** Because the intent is so high, you can often send this traffic directly to a highly optimized landing page that sells the discovery call. The page must be loaded with social proof, client testimonials, and a clear articulation of your unique framework.
3.  **Retargeting:** Not everyone will book immediately. Ensure you have Google Display or Facebook retargeting pixels installed so you can "follow" them around the internet after they leave your page.

## LinkedIn Ads: The B2B Sniper

If you sell B2B coaching services, your ideal clients are on LinkedIn. The targeting capabilities are unparalleled. You can target the exact job title, company size, and seniority level of your ideal prospect.

### Best For:
*   Corporate/Executive Coaches
*   Sales Coaches
*   Leadership Consultants

### The Strategy:
LinkedIn is the most expensive platform on a cost-per-click basis. It is very easy to burn $1,000 in a weekend and get nothing to show for it.

1.  **Lead Gen Forms:** Utilize LinkedIn's native Lead Generation forms. This keeps the user on the platform and automatically pre-fills their information, resulting in much higher conversion rates than sending them to an external landing page.
2.  **Gated High-Value Assets:** Because the audience is professional, they won't opt in for a generic checklist. You need to offer industry reports, proprietary data, or highly exclusive webinars.
3.  **The SDR Follow-Up:** When a lead opts in via LinkedIn, your follow-up needs to be aggressive but professional. A combination of automated emails and direct LinkedIn outreach from your profile is required to book the call.

## YouTube Ads: The Trust Accelerator

YouTube is the ultimate platform for building parasocial relationships at scale. It combines the visual, trust-building nature of television with the targeting capabilities of Google.

### Best For:
*   High-Ticket Coaches in any niche
*   Coaches with a strong on-camera presence

### The Strategy:
YouTube ads (in-stream ads that play before a video) are fantastic for demonstrating your expertise quickly. 

1.  **The First 5 Seconds:** You must hook the viewer immediately before they can click the "Skip Ad" button. Call out your audience directly: "If you are a 6-figure agency owner struggling with churn..."
2.  **The Educational Pitch:** Provide genuine value in the ad itself. Teach them something they didn't know for 2-3 minutes.
3.  **The VSL Funnel:** Drive traffic directly to a longer Video Sales Letter or an automated webinar that further builds authority before pitching the discovery call.

## The Biggest Mistake Coaches Make with Ads

The most common reason coaching ad campaigns fail has nothing to do with the ad platform itself. It is a failure of the **backend infrastructure**.

You can run the best Facebook ad in the world, but if your landing page loads slowly, your copy is confusing, your booking software is glitchy, or your email nurture sequence is non-existent, you will lose the lead. Lead generation is a chain; it is only as strong as its weakest link.
