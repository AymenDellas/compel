  1. Pre-Launch (Teasing the solution and building a waitlist).

## The Anatomy of a Profitable Coaching Launch

Launching a new group coaching program, mastermind, or intensive cohort is one of the most profitable events a coach can run. It allows you to inject a massive amount of cash into your business in a very short window. 

However, a launch is not just an announcement. 

If you just send an email saying, "Hey, my new business coaching program is open, click here to buy," you will fail. A successful launch is an orchestrated event. It is a narrative arc that takes your audience on a journey from unawareness to intense desire. 

When we analyze [why a funnel is leaking](/learn/why-funnel-leaking) during a launch, it usually comes down to pacing. Coaches either sprint (pitching too hard, too fast) or crawl (never actually asking for the sale). 

Whether you are offering [business coaching](/coaches/business-coaching) or launching a life-transformation mastermind, you must follow the laws of human psychology. People need time to warm up to an idea, they need logical proof to justify their emotional desire, and they need a deadline to finally take action.

Here is the exact 10-day, 4-phase email sequence to sell out your next coaching cohort. 

---

## Phase 1: The Pre-Launch (Days 1-3)

**Goal:** Build anticipation, agitate the problem, and segment the most interested buyers onto a waitlist. 

You should not be selling in the pre-launch phase. You are "selling the problem" and hinting at a new solution. You want your audience thinking, "Whatever they are building, I need it."

### Email 1: The "Things Need to Change" Email
**Focus:** Agitate the core problem your target audience is facing right now.
- Start by acknowledging a massive shift in the industry or a common struggle. 
- Explain why the "old way" of solving this problem is broken.
- Tease that you've been working on a new framework behind the scenes to solve this exact issue. 

### Email 2: The "Behind the Scenes" Email
**Focus:** Build authority and show the work. 
- Share the origin story of this new program. Why did you build it? What was the "aha!" moment?
- Show screenshots of your course outline, your notes, or a sneak peek of the framework.
- **The Call to Action:** Ask them to click a link to join the "VIP Early Access Waitlist." (This tags them in your email software as highly engaged).

### Email 3: The Anticipation Builder
**Focus:** Set expectations for the open cart. 
- Announce exactly when the doors will open (e.g., "Tomorrow at 9 AM EST").
- Clearly state the transformation the program will provide.
- Mention that VIP waitlist members will get an early link and a special bonus. 

---

## Phase 2: The Open Cart (Days 4-5)

**Goal:** Generate an immediate influx of sales from your most rabid fans (the "fast action" buyers). 

This is where the energy peaks. Your tone should be excited, confident, and direct. 

### Email 4: The "Doors Are Open!" Email (Morning)
**Focus:** The official announcement. 
- Keep the energy high. "It's finally here!"
- Clearly outline what the program is, who it's for, and the ultimate result they will achieve. 
- Provide a clear, bold link to the sales page or booking calendar. 
- Announce a "Fast Action Bonus" (e.g., a 1-on-1 call with you) for the first 5 people who enroll. 

### Email 5: The FAQ & Logic Email (Next Day)
**Focus:** Transitioning from emotion to logic. 
- The initial hype is over; now, the logical buyers are analyzing the details. 
- Address the most common questions: "How much time does this take?", "Will this work for my specific niche?", "What is the format?"
- Reiterate the core guarantee or [retainer vs performance](/learn/retainer-vs-performance) structure if applicable. 

---

## Phase 3: The Mid-Launch (Days 6-8)

**Goal:** Maintain momentum. The middle of a launch is notorious for a sales "slump." You must keep the energy alive using social proof and objection handling. 

### Email 6: The Case Study / Social Proof Email
**Focus:** Proof that your methodology works.
- Tell a detailed story of a past client who used your framework (the one taught in the new program) to achieve incredible results. 
- Focus heavily on the "Before" state, so the reader sees themselves in the story. 
- The unspoken message is: "If it worked for them, it will work for you."

### Email 7: The "Who is this NOT for?" Email
**Focus:** Creating exclusivity and repelling bad fits. 
- Paradoxically, telling people they might not be a fit increases desire. 
- List 3 traits of people who should *not* join (e.g., "You want a get-rich-quick scheme," "You aren't willing to put in the work").
- Then, list the traits of the ideal client. This challenges the reader to step up and prove they belong. 

### Email 8: The Objection Destroyer 
**Focus:** Tackling the elephant in the room. 
- What is the #1 reason someone won't buy? Is it price? Time? Fear of failure?
- Address it head-on. "You might be thinking you don't have the time for a 6-week mastermind. Let me reframe that..."
- For [CPA and high-ticket](/learn/cpa-high-ticket) offers, this email is critical for justifying the ROI of the investment.

---

## Phase 4: The Close (Days 9-10)

**Goal:** Leverage urgency and scarcity to push the fence-sitters into action. A massive percentage of launch sales occur in the final 24 hours.

### Email 9: The 24-Hour Warning
**Focus:** True scarcity. 
- Remind them that the doors are closing, the bonuses are disappearing, or the price is going up. 
- Summarize the pain of staying where they are versus the pleasure of joining the program. 
- Strong CTA: "Don't miss out. We start next week."

### Email 10: The Final Call (Morning of Cart Close)
**Focus:** Direct, short, urgent. 
- No long stories. 
- "Doors close at midnight tonight. This is your last chance to join this cohort. Here is the link."

### Email 11: The "Last Chance" (3 Hours Before Close)
**Focus:** The final nudge.
- *Subject Line:* Closing in 3 hours. 
- "I'm turning off the checkout page at midnight. If you are on the fence, this is it. See you inside."

---

## Post-Launch: The "Downsell"

Not everyone can afford a $5,000 group coaching program. If you have a lower-tier offer (like a $500 self-paced course or a paid community), wait a few days after the launch ends and send a "Downsell" email to those who didn't buy. 

"I know the Mastermind was a big investment. If you still want to learn the framework but want to do it at your own pace, check out [Lower Tier Offer]."

## Conclusion: Embrace the Volume

The most common reason coaching launches fail is that the coach is afraid to email their list. They send one "doors are open" email and one "doors are closing" email, and wonder why no one bought. 

People are busy. They miss emails. They need to be reminded. If you truly believe your coaching program can change their life, it is your duty to follow up aggressively during a launch window.
