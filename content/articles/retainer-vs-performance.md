---
title: "Retainer vs. Performance Marketing: Which is Best for Coaches?"
date: "2026-06-25"
excerpt: "A breakdown of traditional retainer agencies versus performance-based marketing models for coaching businesses."
---

When scaling a coaching business, one of the biggest decisions you'll face is choosing the right marketing partnership model. The traditional approach is the retainer model, where you pay an agency a fixed monthly fee—often ranging from a few thousand to tens of thousands of dollars—regardless of the results they generate. While this provides predictable expenses and often covers a broad scope of work like brand awareness and social media management, it shifts the financial risk entirely onto your shoulders. If the agency fails to generate qualified leads or book calls, you are still on the hook for their fee, which can quickly drain the capital of a growing coaching practice.

In contrast, performance-based marketing models fundamentally shift this dynamic by aligning the agency's success with your own. In a performance or "pay-on-results" model, you typically pay for tangible outcomes, such as qualified leads generated, booked appointments, or even a percentage of closed revenue. This structure heavily incentivizes the marketing partner to optimize campaigns relentlessly, as their profitability depends entirely on their ability to deliver high-quality prospects to your calendar. For coaches who rely on high-ticket sales calls, this means every marketing dollar spent is directly tied to an opportunity for revenue rather than vanity metrics or vague promises of "brand building."

Ultimately, the choice between these two models comes down to your current business stage and risk tolerance. If you have an established, massive brand and simply need content maintenance and broad visibility, a retainer might make sense. However, for most coaches focused on aggressive growth, maximizing ROI, and protecting their cash flow, a performance-based partnership offers a much safer and more profitable path forward. By paying only for the results that directly impact your bottom line, you can scale your client base with confidence and predictability.
