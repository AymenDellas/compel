## TL;DR: Waking Up the Dead

- **The Problem:** Over time, 20-30% of your email list will go "cold" (they stop opening or clicking your emails). This hurts your deliverability and artificially inflates your software costs. 
- **The Solution:** A 4-part automated Re-Engagement (or "Win-Back") Sequence designed to force a reaction from unengaged subscribers.
- **The Strategy:** Use curiosity, extreme value, and the threat of removal to get them to click a link and prove they are still alive.

## The Hidden Cost of a Cold Email List

Coaches love vanity metrics. It feels good to say, "I have 10,000 people on my email list." But if 4,000 of those people haven't opened an email in six months, you do not have an asset; you have a liability.

A "cold" subscriber is generally defined as someone who has not opened or clicked an email in 90 to 180 days. 

Keeping these cold subscribers on your list has three major hidden costs:

1. **Financial Cost:** Email service providers (like ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit, or Mailchimp) charge you based on the number of contacts on your list. You are literally paying a monthly fee to host email addresses that ignore you.
2. **Deliverability Cost:** This is the most dangerous cost. Email providers like Gmail and Outlook monitor how your audience interacts with your emails. If they see that you consistently send thousands of emails to people who never open them, they will flag your domain as "low engagement." Eventually, they will start sending your emails straight to the Spam or Promotions folder—*even for the people who actually want to read them.*
3. **Analytics Cost:** Cold subscribers ruin your data. They artificially lower your open rates and click-through rates, making it impossible to accurately diagnose [why your funnel is leaking](/learn/why-funnel-leaking). 

Before you delete them, however, you must give them one last chance to stay. This is where the Re-Engagement (or Win-Back) Sequence comes in.

## When to Trigger a Re-Engagement Campaign

You should set up an automation in your email software that automatically triggers when a subscriber hits the 90-day or 120-day mark without opening an email. 

If you are a [career coach](/coaches/career-coaching) or [executive coach](/coaches/executive-coaching), your sales cycles might be slightly longer, so a 120-day trigger is usually appropriate. 

Once they enter this sequence, they stop receiving your regular weekly newsletters. They *only* receive these specific win-back emails.

## The 4-Part Win-Back Sequence

The goal of this sequence is simple: get them to click a specific link. That link will apply a "Re-Engaged" tag in your software, pulling them out of this sequence and putting them back onto your main list. 

### Email 1: The "9-Word Email" Magic Trick
**Goal:** Pure curiosity and pattern interrupt.

This tactic, popularized by real estate investor Dean Jackson, is legendary in direct-response marketing because it looks exactly like a personal email sent from a friend. 

- **Subject Line:** [First Name]
- **Body:** Are you still looking for help with [Specific Problem/Goal]? 

That's it. No logos, no headers, no links. Just a simple question. 
If they reply "Yes," they are immediately re-engaged, and you can transition them into a personal conversation to book a [discovery call](/learn/coaching-discovery-call-script). 

*Example:* "Are you still looking to transition into a Director-level role this year?"

### Email 2: The Extreme Value Bribe
**Goal:** Bribery. Give them something so good they have to click. 
**Send Time:** 3 days after Email 1.

If the short question didn't work, you need to offer immense value. Give them access to a resource you normally charge for, or your absolute best lead magnet.

- **Subject Line:** I made this for you (Free)
- **Body:** Acknowledge that it's been a while since you connected. Tell them you just created a brand new resource (e.g., a masterclass, a new framework template) and you want them to have it for free. 
- **The Catch:** They have to click the link to get it. 
- When they click, your software tags them as re-engaged.

### Email 3: The "Are We Breaking Up?" Email
**Goal:** Emotional pull and direct communication.
**Send Time:** 4 days after Email 2.

This email uses humor and light guilt to get their attention. 

- **Subject Line:** Is it me? 
- **Body:** "Hey [Name], I've noticed you haven't been opening my emails lately. That's totally okay—inboxes get crowded, and maybe [Your Topic] just isn't a priority for you right now. 
But I only want to send emails to people who actually want to receive them. 
If you still want to hear from me, **click here so I know you're still alive**.
If I don't hear from you, I'll assume it's time to part ways and I'll remove you from the list."

### Email 4: The Unsubscribe (The Scrub)
**Goal:** Follow through on your promise.
**Send Time:** 3 days after Email 3.

This is the final email. You are literally telling them you are deleting them. 

- **Subject Line:** Goodbye from [Your Name]
- **Body:** "Hey [Name], as promised, I'm cleaning up my list today. Since I haven't heard from you, I'm officially unsubscribing you. You won't receive any more emails from me.
If this was a mistake and you want to stay, click this link *right now* to stay on the list. Otherwise, I wish you the absolute best with [Their Goal]!"

*Note:* A surprising number of people will click the link on this final email due to loss aversion. Humans hate losing things, even things they were previously ignoring.

## The Beauty of the Scrub

What happens to the people who don't click the link in Email 4?

You delete them. Without hesitation. 

Scrubbing your list is a moment of immense anxiety for most coaches. It hurts to watch your subscriber count drop from 5,000 to 3,500 overnight. 

But you must reframe this. You did not lose 1,500 leads. You removed 1,500 ghosts who were dragging down your deliverability and costing you money. 

The very next time you send an email to your newly cleaned list, your open rates will skyrocket. Your clicks will increase. And because email providers see this massive spike in engagement percentages, your deliverability will improve, meaning more of your warm leads will actually see your emails.
