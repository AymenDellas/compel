## Phase 1: Transforming Your Profile into a Landing Page

When an executive reads your comment or receives a message from you, they will immediately click on your profile. You have approximately three seconds to convince them that you are a peer, an expert, and someone worth their time. 

If your profile looks like you are looking for a job, you will lose them. Your profile must function as a high-converting landing page.

### The Banner and Headshot
Your banner is premium digital real estate. Do not leave it blank or use a generic stock photo of a city skyline. Use this space to clearly state your value proposition, showcase social proof (logos of companies you’ve worked with, or media features), and provide a clear call to action (e.g., "Listen to the latest episode of the Executive Edge Podcast").
Your headshot should be professional, high-resolution, and approachable.

### The Formula for a High-Converting Headline
Your headline is the most visible piece of copy on LinkedIn. It follows you everywhere you go on the platform. Delete "Executive Coach" immediately. It is too generic.

Use this formula: **[Who you help] + [The problem you solve/Outcome you deliver] + [Your Unique Mechanism]**

*   *Weak:* Executive Leadership Coach | ICF Certified
*   *Strong:* Helping Series B SaaS Founders scale their leadership team and navigate hyper-growth | Creator of the 90-Day Alignment Protocol.

### The "About" Section: A Sales Letter, Not a Biography
Do not write your About section in the third person ("John is a seasoned executive..."). Write it directly to your target avatar.
*   **The Hook:** Start by calling out their specific pain points. (e.g., "You successfully closed your Series C, but now your executive team is misaligned, and growth is stalling.")
*   **The Empathy & Authority:** Explain why this happens and why traditional solutions fail. 
*   **The Solution:** Introduce your methodology.
*   **The CTA:** Tell them exactly what to do next. "Book a confidential strategy session here: [Link] or message me 'GROWTH' to chat."

## Phase 2: Inbound Thought Leadership (Content Strategy)

Executives do not hire coaches; they hire authorities. Content is how you demonstrate authority at scale. 

### The Problem with "Inspirational" Content
Many coaches fall into the trap of posting generic leadership advice—"Listen to your employees," or "Empathy is key." While true, this content is commoditized. It doesn't prove that you possess the elite expertise required to justify a $30,000 fee.

### Content Pillars for Executive Coaches
To attract high-level decision-makers, your content must be sharp, specific, and occasionally contrarian. Focus on these pillars:

1.  **Proprietary Frameworks:** Give your methods a name. Explain *how* you solve problems. If you help with team conflict, post an infographic of your "Conflict Resolution Matrix."
2.  **Case Studies and Teardowns:** Deconstruct a famous business failure from a leadership perspective. Or, anonymize a client's success story. ("How we helped a Fortune 500 VP reduce team turnover by 40% in 6 months.")
3.  **Contrarian Perspectives:** Challenge industry norms. ("Why traditional 360 reviews actually destroy team trust.") This polarizes your audience—pushing away bad fits and fiercely attracting your ideal clients.
4.  **Data and Industry Insights:** Synthesize complex trends. If you coach in the healthcare sector, write about the leadership implications of recent regulatory changes.

Consistency is vital. Aim for 3-4 high-quality posts per week.

## Phase 3: Targeted Outbound and Social Selling

Waiting for inbound leads is a luxury. If you want to scale predictably, you must combine your content strategy with a surgical outbound approach. We cover this extensively in our guide to [scaling with LinkedIn](/learn/scaling-linkedin).

### The Perils of Automation
Do not use cheap automation tools to blast hundreds of connection requests with generic pitches. C-suite executives can spot an automated message instantly, and it will permanently damage your brand.

### Leveraging Sales Navigator for "Trigger Events"
Upgrade to LinkedIn Sales Navigator. This is essential. Use it to build highly specific lists (e.g., "Newly promoted COOs in manufacturing companies in the Midwest with over 500 employees").

More importantly, monitor these lists for **Trigger Events**. A trigger event is a signal that an executive might need your help.
*   Did they just get promoted to a C-level role? (They need onboarding coaching).
*   Did their company just announce a major acquisition? (They need integration coaching).
*   Did the company just experience a massive layoff? (They need morale and restructuring coaching).

### The "Value-First" Connection Request
When reaching out based on a trigger event, lead with value, not a pitch.

*   *"Hi [Name], saw the news about your promotion to CMO—congratulations. I work specifically with newly appointed marketing executives to align their teams in the first 90 days. I recently wrote a brief checklist on avoiding the most common missteps in this transition. Happy to send it over if it’s helpful. No pressure either way."*

You are offering a high-value asset without demanding a call. If they accept, you send the asset, which leads them into your funnel. 

## Phase 4: Moving Traffic Off LinkedIn and Into Your Funnel

LinkedIn is rented land. The algorithm can change tomorrow. The ultimate goal of your **executive coaching LinkedIn** strategy is to move prospects off the platform and into your owned ecosystem (your email list and your calendar).

### The "Feature" Section and Lead Magnets
Your featured section should link directly to a high-friction, high-value lead magnet. This should not be a fluffy PDF. It should be a robust whitepaper, an on-demand masterclass, or an ROI calculator. When they click, they land on a conversion-optimized page where they exchange their email for the asset.

Once they are on your list, your automated email nurture sequence takes over. This sequence continues to build trust, share case studies, and consistently offer the opportunity to book a [discovery call](/learn/coaching-discovery-call-script).
